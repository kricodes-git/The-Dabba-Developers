Awareness

India is primarily an agricultural-backed country, with a significant portion in the rural sector. In these areas, low income often leads to misconceptions, especially about menstruation and its management. Despite celebrations marking a girl’s transition into womanhood, these celebrations are often overshadowed by untold taunts and taboos affecting women’s health in rural areas.

While the Indian government has taken steps to spread awareness on menstrual health, lack of education often limits the effectiveness of these initiatives. For instance, the topic is kept so secretive that girls are unaware of what is happening to them, making their first experience terrifying.

Lack of awareness contributes to many issues, including the fact that about 63% of female students in rural areas drop out of school each year when menstruation begins. These factors perpetuate the taboos and challenges surrounding menstrual hygiene and general hygiene in rural communities.
